Greening Spaces:
Adopt-A-Lot

The Adopt-A-Lot project encourages the community to build green spaces and plant trees. This program also involves the removal or trimming of potentially dangerous trees as part of the services they offer in the barangay.

Particularly during storms or high winds, overgrown or insecure trees can threaten lives, damage houses and cars, and more. By this initiative, the barangay promotes responsible tree care in addition to initiatives to expand urban greenery ,ensuring a balance between environmental sustainability and public safety.

HAPAG Project
(Halina’t Magtanim ng Prutas at Gulay)

This project is where the team plants various vegetables and fruits at the place called Echo Park in Amihan, Tagapo.

"HAPAG Project
(Halina’t Magtanim ng Prutas at Gulay)"

The HAPAG Project is where the Barangay Tagapo cleaning team grow fruits and vegetables in Echo Park, Amihan, Tagapo. They support the initiative by preparing planting areas, maintaining garden cleanliness, and assisting in crop care.

This project promotes healthy eating, food sustainability, and the productive use of idle spaces. It also strengthens community involvement by encouraging residents to work together toward a greener environment.

"A small seed today grows into a harvest for the whole community. "

AGF (Aquaponics Garden Facility)

Aside from vegetables and fruits, they are also taking care of fishes in their facility assuring that they maintain them.

AGF (Aquaponics Garden Facility)

AGF is a modern, sustainable system that combines fish-raising and plant-growing in one setup. The cleaning team ensures proper maintenance by checking water quality, feeding the fish, and keeping the tanks clean and balanced.

It provides a steady source of healthy food while promoting eco-friendly, water-efficient farming. The facility also serves as an educational tool, introducing the community to innovative urban agriculture techniques.

"Sustainable farming starts in clean hands and clear water."

Material Recovery Facility

This project is about how our team segregate wastes in places at Tagapo like Anros, Buena Rosa, and Cataquiz where wastes that are still useful are set aside.

Material Recovery
Facility

Barangay Tagapo operates waste segregation sites in Anros, Buena Rosa, and Cataquiz. The cleaning team oversees the sorting of recyclable and reusable materials to reduce environmental waste.

The MRF helps reduce pollution, supports proper waste management, and promotes recycling as a potential livelihood opportunity. It also serves as a model for other communities aiming for cleaner and more sustainable waste practices.

"Clean communities start with mindful segregation."
